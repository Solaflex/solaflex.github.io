Demilitarized Zone (DMZ)

On one of the last weekends, I went to the DMZ. The line between the two Koreas is protected by the Military Police. I booked a 2.5h tour by which we entered the DMZ by bus and visited the most northern observatory of South Korea. Unfortunately, it was a little bit foggy and the cameras were not able to see the North Korean side. Overall, the trip was worthwhile but a lot of history I already knew from my "North Korean socienty and culture" class. Important: The pictures with nearby fences have been made on the southern border of the DMZ. The pictures with distanced fences are from the observatory and show the actual war line inside the DMZ./p>

Chuseok 2021

Dongmak Beach

Both Koreas celebrate Chuseok (harvest celebration) in November. For 2021, this leads to three free days (20/21/22th November). I decided to spend the weekend on a beach near to Seoul with friends from University. While the beach was actually not that nice (I did not plan the tour!), the experience was excellent and I've enjoyed the time off from University. Seoul is almost a Ghost-City because most Koreans leave the capital in order to travel back to their family in the country side.

Due to the strict Corona rules, we had to split our group of six people into two smaller groups with individual Airbnbs. In one of the last slides, you can see the typical bedroom of Koreans. They sleep on the ground with no matress. Poor friends ...

Starfield Library & Bongeunsa Temple

Gangnam District

In the city center, just below the Han river, the Gangnam district is located. It's a fancy district with a lot of international touch. The houses are modern and you often see taller buildings. It can be compared to NYC. The Starfield Library is a famous place in the underground of Gangnam and it's easy to regonize it because of the large bookshelfs. After our visit to the library, we were passing by the Bongeunsa temple and decided to make a short visit.

Dormitory

Ewha Womens University

During the signup process for my Semester abroad, I've chosen to apply for a room in the dormitory. Therefore, on my day of release from the quranatine (25th of August), I moved into the dormitory. It was a stressful check-in because over 100 students had the same plan as I did on this day! The room itself was ok. Compared to my 2-room apartment in Basel it was a little downgrade but the price of 1'800'000 WON (~1600 CHF) for four months is hard to beat. Unfortunately, the dormitory office was disproportional in their effort to battle Corona and decided to introduce stricter rules onto us then the government required. We were basically disallowed to travel outside of Seoul and staying outside longer then 24 hours. These measurements were not known to me beforehand. I therefore decided on the second week to move out of the University's dormitory into my own little studio in Seoul.
Lesson learned: Freedom comes with a cost.
