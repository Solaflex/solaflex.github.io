Student Cafe's

My University semester started earlier than in Switzerland. I was lucky to get all my required classes. Other students were not as successful in getting a seat during the competitive signup process. Starting from 1th of September, my four classes are:

  • Statistics for Business
  • Operational Management
  • Management Information Systems

In Korea, it's common to study and learn in Student cafe's. These places are more welcoming to students who order something and sit for several hours. Due to the small size of my studio, I also prefer to study in this way and melt into the local culture.

Starfield Library & Bongeunsa Temple

Gangnam District

In the city center, just below the Han river, the Gangnam district is located. It's a fancy district with a lot of international touch. The houses are modern and you often see taller buildings. It can be compared to NYC. The Starfield Library is a famous place in the underground of Gangnam and it's easy to regonize it because of the large bookshelfs. After our visit to the library, we were passing by the Bongeunsa temple and decided to make a short visit.

Dormitory

Ewha Womens University

During the signup process for my Semester abroad, I've chosen to apply for a room in the dormitory. Therefore, on my day of release from the quranatine (25th of August), I moved into the dormitory. It was a stressful check-in because over 100 students had the same plan as I did on this day! The room itself was ok. Compared to my 2-room apartment in Basel it was a little downgrade but the price of 1'800'000 WON (~1600 CHF) for four months is hard to beat. Unfortunately, the dormitory office was disproportional in their effort to battle Corona and decided to introduce stricter rules onto us then the government required. We were basically disallowed to travel outside of Seoul and staying outside longer then 24 hours. These measurements were not known to me beforehand. I therefore decided on the second week to move out of the University's dormitory into my own little studio in Seoul.
Lesson learned: Freedom comes with a cost.

Quarantine

G2 Hotel in Seoul

Seoul has seen a steady increase of Corona cases during the summer 2021. My initial hope to get a Quarantine Excemption Certificate was vanished by the time I applied for my visa. The South Korean government was not willing to reduce the rules in favor of foreigners while the Corona cases were increasing. This would have been a suicide mission for every politician. Therefore I had to sit out my 14 nights of mandatory quarantine in the G2 hotel. The food was ok-ish but not enough. It was therefore also a little diet :)
